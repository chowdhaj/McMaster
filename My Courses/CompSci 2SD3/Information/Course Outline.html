Calendar Description

Models of concurrency: process algebras, Petri nets, temporal logics and model checking; concurrency as software structuring principle: processes, threads, synchronization mechanisms, resource management and sharing; deadlock, safety and liveness; design, verification and testing of concurrent systems.
Three lectures, one tutorial (two hours); second term
Prerequisite(s):
COMPSCI 2C03, 2LC3 or 2DM3, 2ME3
Co-requisite(s):
COMPSCI 2AC3
Antirequisite(s):
COMPSCI 3SD3, SFWRENG 3BB4

Course Objectives

Processes, threads, concurrency; Synchronization mechanisms, resource management and sharing; Objects and concurrency; Design, architecture and testing of concurrent systems. The mission of this course is to give students an understanding of the basic techniques of concurrent system design and to introduce students to the appropriate modeling and analysis techniques. A mixture of theory and programming assignments in C (UNIX) and GO. The students learn GO on their own, though several aspects of GO will be discussed in the lectures.

About this course

The course has three hours of lectures and 2 hours of tutorials each week.

For serious reasons (such as academic accommodation, or health issues (MSAF), or participation at an official hackathon out of town) the students may be allowed to

  • submit an assignment late.
  • or submit a make-up assignment instead if a sample solution had been already released
Such action must be arranged with and approved by the instructor at least one week prior to the official deadline for the assignemnt. The electronic submission must be done via the course website.
  • Cooperation among students in the course is strongly encouraged during the the tutorials, however the assignemnts and tests are to be individual effort -- the solution to the problem must be your own to count; solutions designed with help of others (including the ones copied from the internet, or other students, or provided by other means) will be treated as a case of academic dishonesty, see below.
