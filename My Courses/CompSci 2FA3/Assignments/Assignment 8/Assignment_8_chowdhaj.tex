  \item \textbf{[10 points]} Let $G = (N,\Sigma,P,S)$ be the CFG
    where $N = \set{S}$, $\Sigma = \set{a,b}$, and $P$ contains the
    following productions:

  \begin{itemize}

    \item[] $S \rightarrow aSb \mid \epsilon$.

  \end{itemize}

  For $x \in \Sigma^*$, let $Q(x)$ be the property that $S \der{*}{G}
  x$ iff $x = a^nb^n$ for some $n \ge 0$.  Prove \[\ForallApp x \in
  \Sigma^* \mdot Q(x)\] by weak induction on the length of the
  derivation $S \der{*}{G} x$ for the ($\Rightarrow$) direction and by
  strong induction on the length of $x$ for the ($\Leftarrow$)
  direction.

  \bigskip

  \textcolor{blue}{\textbf{Jatin Chowdhary | \textit{chowdhaj} | March 22nd}}

  \textcolor{blue}{\textbf{Put your proof here.}}

  \bigskip

  First step is to prove the base case.

  \textit{Base Case}:

  \begin{itemize}

    \item[] $S \der{1}{G} x$. So $x$ must be $\epsilon$, which makes $x = a^0b^0$ and the result is $\epsilon$, because $a \, \& \, b$ are being repeated 0 times; not repeated at all. Thus, $Q'_{\implies}$(1) holds.

  \end{itemize}

  \bigskip

  Second step is to prove the weak induction step.

  \textit{Weak Induction Step}:

  \begin{itemize}

    \item[] $S \der{n + 1}{G} x$ where $1 \leq n$. I will assume $Q'_{\implies}$(n). Then 
    $S \der{1}{G} \alpha \der{n}{G} x$ where $\alpha = aSb$. Now, $x = ayb$. Since this implies that $S \der{n}{G} y$, the induction hypothesis implies that 
    $y = a^{n-1}b^{n-1}$, which further implies that $x = a^{n}b^{n}$. In conclusion, 
    the statement $Q'_{\implies}$(n + 1) holds.

  \end{itemize}

  $\therefore \forall n \in \mathbb{N}$, the property $Q'_{\implies}$(n) holds.

  \bigskip

  The third step is to prove the strong induction case on the length of $x$

  \item \textbf{[10 points]} Let $\Sigma = (\sB,\sC,\sF,\sP,\tau)$ be
    a signature of MSFOL where:

  \begin{itemize}

    \item[] $\sB = \set{\alpha,\beta}$.

    \item[] $\sC = \set{a,b}$ with $\tau(a) = \alpha$ and $\tau(b) = \beta$.

    \item[] $\sF = \set{f,g}$ with $\tau(f) = \alpha \times \beta
      \rightarrow \alpha$ and $\tau(g) = \beta
      \rightarrow \beta$.

    \item[] $\sP = \set{p,q}$ with $\tau(p) = \alpha \rightarrow
      \mathbb{B}$ and $\tau(q) = \beta \times \beta \rightarrow
      \mathbb{B}$.

  \end{itemize}

  Write a context-free grammar in BNF form that generates the set of
  $\Sigma$-formulas.  Assume $\sV = \set{u,v,w,x,y,z}$.

  \textcolor{blue}{\textbf{Put your BNF here.}}

  $\langle\Sigma-Formula \rangle ::= \langle Negation\rangle \,|\, \langle Implication\rangle \, | \, \langle Equality \rangle$

  $\langle Negation \rangle ::= \langle not \rangle \, \langle\Sigma-Formula\rangle$

  $\langle not \rangle ::= \neg$

  $\langle Implication \rangle ::= \langle\Sigma-Formula\rangle \, \langle Implies \rangle \, \langle\Sigma-Formula\rangle$  

  $\langle Implies \rangle ::= \implies$  

  $\langle Equality \rangle ::= \langle\Sigma-Formula\rangle \, \langle Equals \rangle \, \langle\Sigma-Formula\rangle$  

  $\langle Equals \rangle ::= \,\, \textbf{=}$

  $\langle Boole \rangle ::= true \, | \, false$

  $\langle B-\Sigma-Term \rangle ::= \langle \alpha-term \rangle \, | \, \langle \beta-term \rangle$

  $\langle F-\Sigma-Function \rangle ::= \langle f \rangle \, | \, \langle g \rangle$

  $\langle f \rangle ::= \alpha \times \beta \, \langle Implies \rangle \, \alpha$

  $\langle g \rangle ::= \beta \, \langle Implies \rangle \,  \beta$

  $\langle F-\Sigma-Predicate \rangle ::= \langle p \rangle \, | \, \langle q \rangle$

  $\langle p \rangle ::= \alpha \, \langle Implies \rangle \, \langle Boole \rangle$

  $\langle q \rangle ::= \beta \times \beta \, \langle Implies \rangle \, \langle Boole \rangle$

  $\langle Variable - V \rangle ::= u \, | v \, | w \, | x \, | y | \, z$
